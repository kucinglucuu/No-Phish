Fake Giveaways & Promos:
“Congratulations! You Won a Prize…” as Bait

Educational Goals

Users learn that fake promotions and giveaway scams are a major phishing technique used to gather personal data or login information.

What are Fake Giveaways?

Fake promos are phishing traps that tell users they’ve “won” a prize or free reward to get them to click a link, fill out a form, or submit login info. These are often shared via social media or messaging apps.

  • Bold claims like “You’re our lucky winner!” or “Claim new iPhone now”.
  • Often brand-spoofed with real logos.
  • Link disguises destination with URL shorteners.
  • Requires login, personal details, or even OTPs.

"Fake Cadbury Christmas Giveaway Spreads on WhatsApp: Here’s What You Need to Know"

Scammers spread fake giveaway messages on WhatsApp using a well-known brand name. Victims are asked to click a link and provide personal information to claim a prize, which is then used for fraud and further scam distribution.

Why Do So Many People Get Fooled?

  • People want something for “free”.
  • Real promotions do sometimes happen, so users trust them.
  • The scam may appear endorsed by friends who shared it.
The mindset that Nophish users should cultivate: If something seems too good to be true, it usually is. Always verify from official brand channels, not forwarded links
