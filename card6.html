Fake Online/Google Form Fraud:
“Fill in your details to claim your reward”

Educational Goals

Users know that fake forms (especially Google Forms) are widely used in phishing to harvest personal and login data.

What is a Fraudulent Online Form?

This is a form that pretends to be from a legitimate brand or event, asking for login credentials, personal identification, phone numbers, email, etc. Then sends that data to attackers.

  • Form title mimics known brands (“Claim X Reward”)
  • Asks for sensitive details that real providers wouldn’t request.
  • Shared via messaging groups or social media.
  • Form URL clearly is generic (docs.google.com/forms) but claims official.

"How fraudsters abuse Google Forms to spread scams"

Attackers use fake Google Forms that look legitimate to collect sensitive information such as emails, phone numbers, and login credentials under the disguise of surveys, rewards, or account verification.

Why Do So Many People Get Fooled?

  • Users assume forms from Google are safe.
  • The form design looks legitimate with normal fields.
  • Social proof (“everyone in the group is filling it”).
The mindset that Nophish users should cultivate: Never enter login credentials, OTPs, or banking info into a shared generic form unless it’s verified through official channels.
