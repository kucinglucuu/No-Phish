Recognize Phishing Emails:
Fake Emails That Look Very Convincing

Educational Goals

Users understand that phishing emails are not just bad emails or typos, but many look very similar to official emails, complete with logos, similar domains, and even neat language.

What is a Phishing Email?

Users understand that phishing emails are not just bad emails or typos, but many look very similar to official emails, complete with logos, domains, and messages that seem urgent.

  • Uses logos and designs similar to official emails
  • Uses similar email domains (e.g., @tokoped1a-support.com)
  • Contains urgent messages like “Your account will be blocked in 24 hours!”
  • Fake CTA buttons/links like “Verify Now”

"Fake Emails Using BRI Bank Name Harm Many Customers"

Victims clicked links in fake emails that appeared to be from banks, redirected to fake websites, then drained their funds within minutes.

Why Do So Many People Get Fooled?

  • The email looks professional
  • The victim panics
  • “If this is fake, why does it look real?”
  • People rarely check email address details
The mindset that Nophish users should cultivate: Always check the sender’s email address and the destination link before clicking anything.
