Modus Phising dengan QR Code

Educational Goals

Users learn that phishing attacks have evolved beyond links and emails, QR codes can be weaponized (called quishing) to send victims to fraudulent sites.

What is QR Code Phishing (Quishing)?

Quishing is a phishing technique where attackers use a QR code to redirect victims to a fake login page or malicious site. Since the URL is hidden inside the QR code, many victims are tricked before they even see the link.

  • QR codes appear on posters, flyers, or messages promising rewards, certificates, or event access.
  • Scanning redirects to a site that imitates Google, banking, or platform login pages.
  • After entering credentials, attackers immediately get access.

"Beware of Phishing Under the Guise of Prizes and Fake QR Codes"

News warns of phishing disguised as hadiah (rewards) using QR codes that redirect users to fake login pages.

Why Do So Many People Get Fooled?

  • People assume QR codes are safe because theyre widely used for payments.
  • Victims dont see the actual URL until after scanning.
  • The message uses urgency or rewards to lure victims.
The mindset that Nophish users should cultivate: Don’t trust a QR scan automatically, always check the domain shown after scanning and never enter login credentials on a page you didn’t navigate to intentionally.
