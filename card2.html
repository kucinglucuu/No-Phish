Fake Login Websites:
Similar URLs, Same Appearance, But Not Genuine

Educational Goals

Users understand that phishing doesnt only happen in email, criminals often build fake websites that look almost the same as the real login pages (banking, social media, university portals, etc.) with URLs that look familiar, to trick users into entering credentials.

What is a Fake Login Website?

Fake login websites are crafted to mimic legitimate services (same logo, layout, colors) but are not owned by the real service provider. The main goal is to capture usernames, passwords, and sometimes 2FA codes so attackers can take over accounts.

"Phishing Attack Spoofs Facebook Login Page to Capture Credentials"

Cybercriminals set up fake Facebook login pages to capture credentials from users who thought they were signing in normally.

Why Do So Many People Get Fooled?

  • The fake page looks exactly like the real one
  • People rarely check the URL carefully.
  • The login prompt creates urgency “please re-login to continue”.
  • Users assume if HTTPS lock is present then its safe, even when URL is wrong.
The mindset that Nophish users should cultivate: Before entering login details, always verify the URLs spelling carefully, and consider bookmarking the real site instead of clicking links in messages.
