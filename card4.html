Social Engineering:
Phishing that Exploits Emotions (Panic, Fear, Temptation of Prizes)

Educational Goals

Users learn that phishing isn’t just about tech, attackers use psychological tricks: panic, fear, greed (prizes), authority, or urgency to manipulate victims into acting without thinking.

What is Social Engineering in Phishing?

It’s the art of manipulating human emotions so victims ignore clues and comply with attackers’ requests, whether it’s clicking a link, opening attachments, or entering credentials. Emotional triggers are a key part of modern attacks.

  • Messages claiming “Your account will be closed!” or “You won a prize!”
  • Scare tactics (“Unauthorized login attempt!”).
  • Time-limited offers to create rush decisions.
  • Using official logo, authority figures, or legal-sounding text.

"Understanding Phishing, a Fraud Method That Often Increases During Eid"

Phishing scams spike during festive periods, exploiting emotions and urgency to lure victims. Microsoft Indonesia has urged users to stay alert especially during Lebaran period when phishing increases.

Why Do So Many People Get Fooled?

  • Fear or excitement overrides caution.
  • Most users are trained to comply quickly in stressful or reward situations.
  • Attackers use realistic branding and urgent messaging.
The mindset that Nophish users should cultivate: Always pause and verify, urgent language is a red flag. Ask: “Does this message make sense from this sender?”
